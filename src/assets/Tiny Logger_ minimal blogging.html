After I graduated from college, I applied to every investment banking job I could. I emailed every alum in the industry, repeatedly. This was before I found Reddit, and I was a regular on a forum called Wall Street Oasis. I soaked up all the advice I could on breaking into the industry, which boiled down to "know your stuff" and "stay persistent until someone gives you a chance." In short, for about a year and a half, I was obsessed with everything related to investment banking.

90% of my emails didn't get a response. Although the forums warned me this would happen, after months of reaching out, I was pretty discouraged. And then, one day, some mid-level Wall Street grunt shared posted a message that fundamentally changed the way I think about communication. It went something like:

> Most of your messages won't get responses, but know that **every email you send is read**. I see them pop up in my inbox. Often, I'm too busy to respond right away. Then I forget, so sometimes I appreciate when you follow up. Sometimes I know I'm can't help, so I don't respond. I know it's demoralizing to keep reaching out, and it can make you feel like you're shouting into the void. But you're not.

It's now ten years later and although banking is no longer a part of my life, but this lesson stayed with me. I used to think that if my message didn't get a response, it was a personal rejection. Now I find fulfillment in crafting and sending the message. I trust it'll be read, even if I never hear about it again.

And I've taken it easier on myself, too, because I also get meaningful messages that I want to respond to. I used to try to get back to people right away, and I'd sometimes get stuck in a cycle of guilt and procrastination trying to craft the perfect response. Now I cut myself some slack, and savor the things people say. I try to get back to them when I can, and I accept that my responses will sometimes fall short of what I want to convey.

It's not perfect; I'm sure most people dislike not hearing back. But I find real comfort in knowing that every message I send is read, even if I don't get a response.
